Implicit Bias: Quick Guide

We put this guide together to make terms like implicit bias and intersectionality easy for first-year students to understand. Here are simple definitions and links if you want to learn more.

What is Implicit Bias?

Implicit bias refers to automatic, often unconscious associations that can influence how we see and treat others. Everyone has them; the work is to notice and reduce their impact.

Intersectionality

Intersectionality looks at how parts of identity (race, gender, class, disability, sexuality, etc.) overlap to shape experience. It helps us avoid one-size-fits-all assumptions and design more inclusive solutions.

Implicit Bias Test (IBT)

The IBT (from Project Implicit) helps reveal automatic associations. Treat results as a starting point for reflection, not a label. Use them to set small goals and track change over time.

First Steps to Act

  • After an IBT, write one surprise and one behavior change to try this week.
  • In projects, ask: “Who might be excluded?” and “How can we adapt?”
  • Use inclusive language; invite feedback; keep learning.
